ECLOGUE X.

THE elegiae poet Cornelius Gallus, a friend of virgil, had been des-
patched (apparently) to defend the Italian water-s from the freebooting
squadron of Sextus Pompey. In his absence, his mistress—here spoken
of under the name Lycoris—had been unfaithful to him, and had fol-
lowed a soldier of Agrippa's army into Gaul (uc. 37); and he requested
of Virgil a pastoral poem, which ”might have the good luck to win him
back his love. The poem is a free imitation of the first idyl of Theocritus.
